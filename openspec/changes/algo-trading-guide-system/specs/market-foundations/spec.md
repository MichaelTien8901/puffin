## ADDED Requirements

### Requirement: Market structure education
The system SHALL provide tutorial content explaining how financial markets work, including exchanges, order books, bid-ask spreads, and market participants.

#### Scenario: Order book explanation
- **WHEN** a learner reads the market structure chapter
- **THEN** they find explanations of limit orders, market orders, bid-ask spread, and order book depth with visual diagrams

### Requirement: Asset class coverage
The system SHALL cover equities, ETFs, and cryptocurrency as primary asset classes with explanations of their characteristics and trading mechanics.

#### Scenario: Asset class comparison
- **WHEN** a learner reads the asset classes chapter
- **THEN** they find a comparison of equities, ETFs, and crypto covering trading hours, settlement, fees, and volatility characteristics

### Requirement: Trading mechanics tutorial
The system SHALL explain order types (market, limit, stop, stop-limit), execution mechanics, slippage, and commission structures.

#### Scenario: Order type walkthrough
- **WHEN** a learner reads the trading mechanics chapter
- **THEN** they find interactive examples showing how each order type behaves in different market conditions

### Requirement: Key financial metrics education
The system SHALL teach fundamental financial metrics: returns, volatility, Sharpe ratio, drawdown, alpha, and beta.

#### Scenario: Metrics with code examples
- **WHEN** a learner reads the financial metrics chapter
- **THEN** they find Python code examples calculating each metric from sample market data

### Requirement: Historical context
The system SHALL provide context on market history, notable events (flash crashes, regime changes), and why they matter for algorithmic trading.

#### Scenario: Historical events coverage
- **WHEN** a learner reads the historical context chapter
- **THEN** they find case studies of at least 3 significant market events and their implications for automated trading systems
